Here are prevention methods for various diseases across different categories:

Infectious Diseases Influenza (Flu) Annual vaccination Good hygiene (handwashing, using hand sanitizer) Avoid close contact with sick individuals COVID-19 Vaccination Wearing masks in crowded places Practicing good hand hygiene Social distancing Tuberculosis (TB) Vaccination (BCG vaccine) Regular screening for at-risk populations Ensuring proper ventilation in living spaces Chronic Diseases Heart Disease Healthy diet (low in saturated fats, cholesterol, and sodium) Regular physical activity Avoiding smoking and excessive alcohol consumption Regular health screenings (blood pressure, cholesterol levels) Diabetes Maintaining a healthy weight Regular exercise Balanced diet rich in whole grains, fruits, and vegetables Regular blood sugar monitoring for at-risk individuals Cancer Breast Cancer Regular mammograms and clinical breast exams Self-exams for early detection Healthy lifestyle choices (diet, exercise, limited alcohol) Cervical Cancer HPV vaccination Regular Pap smears and HPV testing Safe sexual practices Mental Health Depression and Anxiety Regular physical activity Healthy diet Stress management techniques (mindfulness, meditation) Seeking help when needed (therapy, support groups) Vector-borne Diseases Malaria Use of insecticide-treated bed nets Indoor spraying with insecticides Antimalarial medications for travelers to high-risk areas Dengue Fever Eliminating standing water to reduce mosquito breeding Using mosquito repellent Wearing long sleeves and pants in endemic areas General Preventive Measures Vaccinations: Stay updated on recommended vaccines. Healthy Lifestyle: Maintain a balanced diet, regular exercise, and adequate sleep. Regular Screenings: Participate in health screenings for early detection of diseases. Education: Stay informed about health risks and preventive measures.
